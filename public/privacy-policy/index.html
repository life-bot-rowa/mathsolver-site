By using the Services, you acknowledge that you have read this Privacy Policy and agree to the practices described below. If you do not agree, please do not use the Services.

1. Scope

This Policy applies to:

  • The MathSolver website at https://mathsolver.cloud and all of its subpages;
  • The Math Solver Chrome extension distributed through the Chrome Web Store;
  • Any mobile or desktop applications we publish under the Math Solver brand in the future;
  • Support, sales, and marketing communications we send you.

This Policy does not apply to third-party websites, services, or products that we do not own or control, even when we link to them.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Information You Provide Directly

  • Contact form: first name, last name, email address, message content.
  • Account data (when you sign up for a paid plan): email address, authentication credentials (stored as a hashed token), subscription tier and status.
  • Support correspondence: any information you choose to share when you email us or reply to our messages.
  • Survey and feedback responses, if you choose to participate.

2.2 Payment Information (Processed by Paddle)

All payments on the Services are processed by our payment provider and Merchant of Record, Paddle.com Market Limited and its affiliates (“Paddle”). When you purchase a subscription, Paddle collects and processes:

  • Billing name and country;
  • Billing address (where required by tax law);
  • Payment card details or alternative payment method details;
  • Transaction metadata (amount, currency, date, order ID).

We do not receive or store your full payment card number. We receive from Paddle only the minimum information needed to fulfill your order and comply with tax and accounting obligations (e.g., order ID, masked card brand and last four digits, billing country, invoice amount).

Paddle’s handling of your payment data is governed by Paddle’s own Privacy Policy, available at: https://www.paddle.com/legal/privacy

2.3 Information Collected via the Chrome Extension

When you install and use the Math Solver Chrome extension, we process the following:

  • Screenshots of math problems you explicitly submit. The extension captures an image only when you actively trigger it (for example, by selecting an area of the screen and pressing the solve button). The captured image is sent to our AI solving backend to generate a step-by-step solution and is returned to you in the extension UI.
  • Solution requests metadata: timestamp, extension version, anonymous request ID, and result status (success / failure). This is used for quality control, rate limiting, and debugging.
  • Authentication token (only if you are signed in to a paid subscription) so that your plan entitlement can be verified.
  • Basic device and browser information: Chrome version, operating system, and extension language — used for compatibility and error diagnosis.

What the extension does NOT do:

  • It does not read page content, forms, passwords, or browsing history;
  • It does not access tabs you have not explicitly captured;
  • It does not record your screen continuously;
  • It does not activate without your action;
  • It does not access your files, camera, microphone, or location.

2.4 Information Collected Automatically on the Website

When you visit mathsolver.cloud, we and our service providers may automatically collect:

  • IP address, approximate location (country / city inferred from IP);
  • Browser type and version, operating system, device type;
  • Referring URL, pages viewed, time on page, clicks, scroll depth;
  • Error logs and performance data;
  • Cookie identifiers and similar tracking technologies (see Section 7).

2.5 Information from Third Parties

We may receive information from:

  • Paddle (order confirmations, fraud signals, refund events);
  • Google (when you install the extension from the Chrome Web Store — we receive install counts, review data, and anonymous ratings);
  • Analytics providers (aggregated statistics about site usage).

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

  • To provide the Services: solve math problems submitted via the extension, deliver step-by-step explanations, operate your account, and enforce subscription entitlements.
  • To process payments: work with Paddle to accept, verify, and fulfill your order, issue invoices, and handle refunds.
  • To communicate with you: send transactional emails (receipts, subscription reminders, password resets, policy updates), respond to support requests, and — only with your consent — send marketing emails and product updates. You can unsubscribe from marketing emails at any time.
  • To improve the Services: analyze usage patterns, measure feature performance, fix bugs, and develop new features.
  • To protect the Services and our users: detect and prevent fraud, abuse, spam, and security incidents; rate-limit excessive or abusive requests.
  • To comply with legal obligations: respond to lawful requests from authorities, meet tax and accounting requirements, and resolve disputes.

We do not use your information for automated decision-making that produces legal or similarly significant effects about you.

4. Legal Basis for Processing (EEA / UK / Switzerland)

If you are located in the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, or Switzerland, we process your personal data on the following legal bases:

  • Performance of a contract — to provide the Services you requested and process your subscription;

  • Consent — for optional marketing communications and non-essential cookies. You may withdraw your consent at any time without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing;
  • Legal obligation — to comply with applicable tax, accounting, and regulatory requirements.

7. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

We use cookies and similar technologies on mathsolver.cloud for the following purposes:

  • Strictly necessary cookies — required for the Services to function (e.g., session management, checkout flow with Paddle, CSRF protection);
  • Analytics cookies — Google Analytics cookies that help us understand traffic and usage (these are set only after you consent, where required by law);
  • Payment cookies — set by Paddle during the checkout process to prevent fraud and complete transactions.

You can manage or disable cookies through your browser settings. Disabling strictly necessary cookies may break core features such as checkout and authentication.

The Math Solver browser extension itself does not set cookies on the pages you visit.
