1

Step 1: Understanding the Problem

The first step in solving algebra problems is comprehending what the problem is asking. Carefully read the given equation or word problem and identify the variable you need to solve for. Look for keywords or phrases that indicate mathematical operations, such as "sum," "difference," "product," or "quotient." Understanding the problem is crucial for setting up the correct equation.

2

Step 2: Setting Up the Equation

Once you understand the problem, the next step is to translate it into a mathematical equation. If the problem is a word problem, identify numbers, variables, and operations needed, then write them in the algebraic equation format. Ensure all terms are correctly positioned, and the equation accurately reflects the problem statement.

3

Step 3: Solving the Equation

With the equation set up, begin solving it by isolating the variable on one side. Use inverse operations to simplify the equation, such as adding or subtracting terms from both sides, and dividing or multiplying as necessary. Keep the equation balanced by performing the same operation on both sides until the variable is isolated.

4

Step 4: Checking Your Work

After finding the solution, it’s important to verify its correctness. Substitute the solution back into the original equation to ensure it satisfies the equation. If the left-hand side equals the right-hand side after substitution, the solution is correct. This step helps confirm accuracy and improves confidence in problem-solving.

Problem: Solve for x: 3x + 5 = 20
