Step-by-Step: How to Solve Step By Step Two Step Equations

1

Step 1: Simplify the Equation

The first step in solving step by step two step equations is to simplify the equation if necessary. This involves combining like terms and eliminating any parentheses by distributing any coefficients. This step ensures that the equation is in its simplest form before you begin solving for the variable. By simplifying the equation, you make it easier to identify the operations needed to isolate the variable.

2

Step 2: Undo Addition or Subtraction

Once the equation is simplified, the next step is to undo any addition or subtraction to begin isolating the variable. Look for any constant term added or subtracted from the variable term and perform the opposite operation on both sides of the equation. This step moves the constant term to the opposite side of the equation, bringing you closer to isolating the variable.

3

Step 3: Undo Multiplication or Division

After addressing addition or subtraction, the next step is to undo any multiplication or division involving the variable. This is done by performing the opposite operation on both sides of the equation. If the variable is multiplied by a coefficient, divide both sides by that coefficient. If the variable is divided, multiply both sides by the denominator. This step will completely isolate the variable, allowing you to find its value.

4

Step 4: Verify the Solution

The final step is to verify your solution by substituting the value of the variable back into the original equation. This ensures that the solution satisfies the equation. If both sides of the equation are equal after substitution, you have correctly solved the equation. Verifying your solution helps catch any arithmetic errors and confirms the accuracy of your answer.

Worked Examples

Example 1

Problem: Solve for x: 3x + 5 = 20
Step 1: Undo Addition: Begin by subtracting 5 from both sides to isolate the term with the variable. 3x + 5 - 5 = 20 - 5 3x = 15
Step 2: Undo Multiplication: Divide both sides by 3 to solve for x. 3x/3 = 15/3 x = 5
Step 3: Verify the Solution: Substitute x = 5 back into the original equation to check. 3(5) + 5 = 20 15 + 5 = 20 20 = 20 The solution is verified, x = 5 is correct.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

One common mistake students make when solving step by step two step equations is neglecting to perform the same operation on both sides of the equation. This can lead to an incorrect solution because it disrupts the balance of the equation. Always remember to perform each step equally on both sides to maintain the equation's integrity.

Another frequent error is miscalculating basic arithmetic operations, such as addition, subtraction, multiplication, or division. Double-check your calculations at each step to avoid this pitfall. Slow down and ensure that each arithmetic operation is performed accurately to prevent small errors from leading to an incorrect final solution.

Frequently Asked Questions

❓ How do step by step two step equations differ from multi step equations?
Step by step two step equations involve solving for a variable using two operations, typically one addition/subtraction and one multiplication/division. Multi step equations, on the other hand, may require more than two steps and involve additional complexities such as distributing variables or combining like terms multiple times.
❓ Why do students find step by step two step equations challenging?
Students may find these equations challenging because they require understanding and executing multiple operations in sequence. Additionally, maintaining the balance of the equation while performing operations on both sides can be confusing without a strong grasp of basic arithmetic and algebraic principles.

Step by step two step equations are a subset of linear equations, which are equations of the first degree. Understanding two step equations lays the foundation for solving more complex linear equations, where multiple variables and operations come into play.
