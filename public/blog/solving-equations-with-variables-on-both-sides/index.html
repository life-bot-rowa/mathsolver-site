Step-by-Step: How to Solve Solving Equations With Variables On Both Sides

1

Step 1: Simplify Each Side of the Equation

Begin by simplifying both sides of the equation separately. This means combining like terms and simplifying any arithmetic expressions. If there are parentheses, apply the distributive property to eliminate them. For example, in the equation 3x + 5 = 2(x + 3) + 1, you'd first distribute the 2 to get 3x + 5 = 2x + 6 + 1. Then, combine like terms to simplify further to 3x + 5 = 2x + 7.

2

Step 2: Get All Variable Terms on One Side

Next, choose a side to keep the variable terms and move all variable terms to that side. This usually involves adding or subtracting terms from both sides of the equation. Continuing with our example, subtract 2x from both sides to get 3x - 2x + 5 = 7, which simplifies to x + 5 = 7.

3

Step 3: Isolate the Variable

With the variable terms on one side, focus on isolating the variable. This often involves getting rid of any constants on the side with the variable. You can do this by adding or subtracting the constant from both sides. In our example, subtract 5 from both sides to isolate the variable: x + 5 - 5 = 7 - 5, resulting in x = 2.

4

Step 4: Verify Your Solution

To ensure the accuracy of your solution, substitute the value of the variable back into the original equation. This step checks if both sides of the equation are equal, confirming the solution's correctness. Substitute x = 2 back into the original equation: 3(2) + 5 = 2(2) + 7. Both sides equal 11, verifying that x = 2 is indeed correct.

Worked Examples

Example 1

Problem: Let's solve the equation: 3x + 4 = 2x + 10.
Step 1: Move all variable terms to one side: Subtract 2x from both sides: 3x - 2x + 4 = 2x - 2x + 10. This simplifies to x + 4 = 10.
Step 2: Isolate the variable: Subtract 4 from both sides to isolate x: x + 4 - 4 = 10 - 4. This gives x = 6.
Step 3: Verify the solution: Substitute x = 6 back into the original equation: 3(6) + 4 = 2(6) + 10. Both sides equal 22, confirming that x = 6 is the correct solution.

Example 2

Problem: Now, solve the equation: 5y - 3 = 2(y + 7) + 4.
Step 1: Simplify the equation: Distribute the 2 on the right side: 5y - 3 = 2y + 14 + 4. Combine like terms to get 5y - 3 = 2y + 18.
Step 2: Move all variable terms to one side: Subtract 2y from both sides: 5y - 2y - 3 = 2y - 2y + 18. This simplifies to 3y - 3 = 18.
Step 3: Isolate the variable: Add 3 to both sides: 3y - 3 + 3 = 18 + 3, giving 3y = 21. Divide by 3 to solve for y: y = 21/3, which simplifies to y = 7.
Step 4: Verify the solution: Substitute y = 7 back into the original equation: 5(7) - 3 = 2(7 + 7) + 4. Both sides equal 32, confirming that y = 7 is correct.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

One common mistake when solving equations with variables on both sides is neglecting to perform the same operation on both sides of the equation. This error can lead to incorrect solutions because it violates the principle of maintaining equality. To avoid this, always double-check that every step maintains balance by applying operations uniformly to both sides.

Another frequent error involves mishandling negative signs, especially when distributing or combining like terms. Students might forget to distribute a negative sign across terms inside parentheses, leading to incorrect simplifications. Carefully track signs throughout your work, and consider rechecking each step to ensure accuracy.
