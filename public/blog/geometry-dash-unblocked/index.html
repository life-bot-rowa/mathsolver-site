Worked Examples

Example 1

Problem: A square has a diagonal of 10 cm. Find the side length and area of the square.
Step 1: Use the Pythagorean theorem: In a square, the diagonal forms a right-angled triangle with two sides of the square. Let the side length be "s." According to the theorem, s^2 + s^2 = 10^2.
Step 2: Simplify: 2s^2 = 100.
Step 3: Solve for s^2: s^2 = 100/2 = 50.
Step 4: Find the side length: s = sqrt(50) ≈ 7.07 cm.
Step 5: Calculate the area: Area = s^2 = 50 cm^2.
