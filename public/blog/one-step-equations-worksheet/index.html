By mastering one step equations, students build a strong foundation for tackling more complex algebraic problems, such as two step equations and multi step equations. Step-by-Step: How to Solve One Step Equations Worksheet

1

Step 1: Understand the Equation

The first step in solving a one step equation is to understand the structure of the equation. A one step equation typically involves a single operation applied to a variable. For instance, in the equation x + 5 = 10, the operation is addition. Recognizing the operation is crucial because it determines the inverse operation required to solve the equation. Take a moment to identify whether the equation involves addition, subtraction, multiplication, or division.

2

Step 2: Identify the Inverse Operation

Once you've identified the operation in the equation, the next step is to determine the inverse operation. The inverse operation is what you'll use to isolate the variable. For example, if the operation in the equation is addition, the inverse operation is subtraction. Similarly, if the equation involves multiplication, the inverse operation would be division. Recognizing the appropriate inverse operation is key to solving one step equations accurately.

3

Step 3: Apply the Inverse Operation

After identifying the inverse operation, apply it to both sides of the equation. This step is crucial because it maintains the balance of the equation. For instance, if the equation is x + 5 = 10, subtract 5 from both sides to isolate x. The goal is to isolate the variable on one side of the equation, leaving the solution on the other side. Ensure that you perform the same operation on both sides to keep the equation balanced.

4

Step 4: Verify Your Solution

The final step is to verify your solution. After isolating the variable, substitute your solution back into the original equation to ensure it satisfies the equation. For example, if you solve x + 5 = 10 and find x = 5, substitute 5 back into the equation to check: 5 + 5 = 10, which is correct. Verifying your solution helps confirm that you have solved the equation correctly and boosts your confidence in handling similar problems.

Worked Examples

Example 1

Problem: Solve for x: 3x + 7 = 22
Step 1: Understand the equation: The equation involves addition after multiplying the variable by 3.
Step 2: Identify the inverse operation: To isolate 3x, subtract 7 from both sides.
Step 3: Apply the inverse operation: 3x + 7 - 7 = 22 - 7 simplifies to 3x = 15.
Step 4: Apply the inverse operation again: Divide both sides by 3 to isolate x. Thus, x = 15/3.
Step 5: Solution: x = 5.
Step 6: Verify: Substitute x = 5 back into the original equation: 3(5) + 7 = 15 + 7 = 22, which is correct.

Example 2

Problem: Solve for y: 5y - 12 = 3y + 8
Step 1: Understand the equation: This equation involves subtraction and requires rearranging terms.
Step 2: Rearrange: Subtract 3y from both sides: 5y - 3y - 12 = 8 simplifies to 2y - 12 = 8.
Step 3: Identify the inverse operation: Add 12 to both sides to isolate the term with y.
Step 4: Apply the inverse operation: 2y - 12 + 12 = 8 + 12 simplifies to 2y = 20.
Step 5: Solve for y: Divide both sides by 2 to isolate y. Thus, y = 20/2.
Step 6: Solution: y = 10.
Step 7: Verify: Substitute y = 10 back into the original equation: 5(10) - 12 = 3(10) + 8, which simplifies to 50 - 12 = 30 + 8, both equal to 38, confirming the solution.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

A frequent mistake students make when solving one step equations is neglecting to apply the inverse operation to both sides of the equation. This oversight can lead to incorrect solutions, as the balance of the equation is disrupted. Always remember that whatever operation you perform on one side of the equation, you must also perform on the other side. This ensures the equation remains balanced and the solution is accurate.

Another common error is misidentifying the operation present in the equation. For instance, students might confuse multiplication with addition, leading to incorrect choice of the inverse operation. Carefully analyze the equation to determine the operation before proceeding with the solution. Practicing with a variety of one step equations worksheets pdf can help reinforce these concepts and reduce mistakes.
