Worked Examples

Example 1

Problem: Let's solve the problem of finding the perimeter and semi-perimeter of triangle ABC, which has sides of 7 cm, 8 cm, and 9 cm.
Step 1: Find the perimeter by adding all the sides: 7 + 8 + 9 = 24 cm. This is the total length around the triangle.
Step 2: Calculate the semi-perimeter by dividing the perimeter by 2. So, 24/2 = 12 cm. The semi-perimeter is useful in further calculations, such as finding the area using Heron's formula. Therefore, the perimeter of triangle ABC is 24 cm, and the semi-perimeter is 12 cm.

Example 2

Problem: Now, consider a rhombus with diagonals measuring 10 cm and 24 cm. We need to find the side length and area.
Step 1: To find the side length, use the fact that each diagonal splits the rhombus into four right triangles. Using the Pythagorean theorem, for one of these triangles: side^2 = (10/2)^2 + (24/2)^2 = 5^2 + 12^2 = 25 + 144 = 169. Thus, side = sqrt(169) = 13 cm.
Step 2: For the area, use the formula (1/2) * diagonal1 * diagonal2. Substitute the diagonals: (1/2) * 10 * 24 = 120 cm^2. Hence, the side length of the rhombus is 13 cm, and the area is 120 cm^2.
