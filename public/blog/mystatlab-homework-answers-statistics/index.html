Worked Examples

Example 1

Problem: A survey was conducted among 50 students to determine their favorite type of music. 20 students preferred pop, 15 preferred rock, and 15 preferred classical. What percentage of students preferred rock music?
Step 1: To find the percentage of students who preferred rock music, we use the formula: Percentage = (number of students preferring rock / total number of students) x 100.
Step 2: Here, the number of students preferring rock is 15, and the total number of students is 50.
Step 3: So, Percentage = (15/50) x 100 = 30%.
Step 4: Therefore, 30% of the students preferred rock music.

Example 2

Problem: In a statistics class, the final exam scores of 30 students were recorded as follows: 88, 92, 76, 85, 90, 78, 95, 83, 80, 94, 87, 91, 82, 89, 77, 84, 86, 80, 92, 81, 93, 79, 88, 75, 90, 86, 91, 84, 87, 92. Calculate the mean and standard deviation of the exam scores.
Step 1: First, calculate the mean: Mean = (sum of all scores) / (number of scores).
Step 2: The sum of all scores is 2520.
Step 3: Thus, Mean = 2520 / 30 = 84.
Step 4: Next, calculate the standard deviation: find the variance by calculating the average of the squared differences from the mean.
Step 5: Variance = sum of (each score - mean)^2 / number of scores.
Step 6: Standard deviation is the square root of the variance.
Step 7: After calculations, you find the standard deviation is approximately 6.05.
