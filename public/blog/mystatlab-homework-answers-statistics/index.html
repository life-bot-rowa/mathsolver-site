Worked Examples

Example 1

Problem: Calculate the mean of the following dataset: 5, 10, 15, 20, 25.
Step 1: Identify the formula for the mean: Mean = ΣX / N. Step 2: Sum the data points: 5 + 10 + 15 + 20 + 25 = 75. Step 3: Count the number of data points: N = 5. Step 4: Apply the formula: Mean = 75 / 5 = 15. Thus, the mean of the dataset is 15.

Example 2

Problem: A dataset contains the numbers: 8, 12, 15, 20, 25, and 30. Calculate the mean and interpret the result.
Step 1: Use the mean formula: Mean = ΣX / N. Step 2: Add the data points: 8 + 12 + 15 + 20 + 25 + 30 = 110. Step 3: Determine the number of data points: N = 6. Step 4: Compute the mean: Mean = 110 / 6 ≈ 18.33. The mean of the dataset is approximately 18.33, indicating that, on average, the data points are centered around this value.
