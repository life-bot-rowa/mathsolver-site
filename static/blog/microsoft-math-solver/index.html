Worked Examples

Example 1

Problem: What is the result of 25 + 36 - 14 / 2?
Step 1: Start with the division: 14 / 2 = 7.
Step 2: Replace the division in the original equation: 25 + 36 - 7.
Step 3: Perform the addition: 25 + 36 = 61.
Step 4: Finally, complete the subtraction: 61 - 7 = 54.

Example 2

Problem: If a triangle has a base of 10 cm and a height of 6 cm, what is the area, and if the area is then multiplied by 3 and divided by the square root of 9, what is the final result?
Step 1: Calculate the area of the triangle: Area = 1/2 * base * height = 1/2 * 10 * 6 = 30 square cm.
Step 2: Multiply the area by 3: 30 * 3 = 90.
Step 3: Divide this result by the square root of 9: sqrt(9) = 3, so 90 / 3 = 30.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

One common mistake students make when using Microsoft Math Solver is entering problems incorrectly. This can lead to incorrect solutions, so it's crucial to double-check your input before proceeding. Ensure that all parentheses and mathematical symbols are used correctly, as missing or misplacing them can alter the problem's meaning.

Another frequent error is relying solely on the tool for answers without understanding the solution process. While Microsoft Math Solver provides detailed steps, it's important to study these explanations to truly grasp the underlying mathematical concepts. This understanding will be invaluable when you encounter similar problems without access to the tool.
