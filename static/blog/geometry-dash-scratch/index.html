Worked Examples

Example 1

Problem: A triangle has a base of 8 units and a height of 5 units. What is the area of the triangle?
Step 1: Identify the formula: The area of a triangle is calculated using the formula: area = 0.5 * base * height.
Step 2: Substitute the values: Here, the base is 8 units, and the height is 5 units. Substitute these values into the formula: area = 0.5 * 8 * 5.
Step 3: Perform the calculation: Multiply 8 by 5, which equals 40. Then, multiply 0.5 by 40 to get 20.
Step 4: Conclude: The area of the triangle is 20 square units.

Example 2

Problem: A rectangle has a length of 12 units and a width of 9 units. If you increase the length by 3 units and decrease the width by 2 units, what will be the new area of the rectangle?
Step 1: Identify the original dimensions: The original dimensions are a length of 12 units and a width of 9 units.
Step 2: Adjust the dimensions: Increase the length by 3 units, resulting in a new length of 15 units. Decrease the width by 2 units, resulting in a new width of 7 units.
Step 3: Calculate the new area: The area of a rectangle is calculated using the formula: area = length * width. Substitute the new dimensions: area = 15 * 7.
Step 4: Perform the calculation: Multiply 15 by 7 to get 105.
Step 5: Conclude: The new area of the rectangle is 105 square units.
