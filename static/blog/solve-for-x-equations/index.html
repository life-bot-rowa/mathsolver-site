Step-by-Step: How to Solve Solve For X Equations

1

Step 1: Simplify Both Sides of the Equation

Before attempting to isolate x, ensure that both sides of the equation are as simple as possible. This may involve distributing any coefficients across parentheses or combining like terms. Simplification reduces the complexity of the equation, making it easier to work with in subsequent steps.

2

Step 2: Move the Variable Terms to One Side

The next step is to get all terms containing x on one side of the equation. Use addition or subtraction to move terms across the equal sign. Remember, whatever operation you perform on one side must also be done to the other side to maintain balance.

3

Step 3: Isolate the Variable

Once all x terms are on one side, isolate x by dividing or multiplying to remove coefficients. If x is multiplied by a number, divide both sides by that number. Conversely, if x is divided by a number, multiply both sides to free x.

4

Step 4: Verify Your Solution

After finding a value for x, substitute it back into the original equation to ensure it satisfies the equation. This verification step is crucial for confirming that no errors were made during the solving process.

Worked Examples

Example 1

Problem: Solve for x: 3x + 5 = 20
Step 1: Subtract 5 from both sides: 3x + 5 - 5 = 20 - 5, which simplifies to 3x = 15.
Step 2: Divide both sides by 3 to isolate x: 3x/3 = 15/3, resulting in x = 5.
Step 3: Verify by substituting x = 5 back into the original equation: 3(5) + 5 = 20, which holds true.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

One common mistake students make is neglecting to perform operations on both sides of the equation. This oversight can lead to incorrect solutions. Always ensure that any addition, subtraction, multiplication, or division is applied equally to both sides of the equation.

Another frequent error is incorrect distribution of coefficients across terms within parentheses. Double-check your work by ensuring the multiplication is applied to each term inside the parentheses. Overlooking this step can lead to inaccurate simplification and, ultimately, the wrong answer.
